Department Description

The Opportunity Consultant shall be a filed deployed/customer facing position that works collaboratively with the On Demand, License, and

Consulting sales teams to win On Demand’s largest and most complex On Demand sales opportunities. The Opportunity Consultant’s primary

responsibility is take a holistic view of the opportunity and coordinate a cross functional deal team (Opportunity Consultant, Solution Manager,

Technical Architects) that must create and deliver the value proposition, the business case, negotiation, pricing, and terms and conditions that

will attempts to maximize Oracle’s financial outcome with the lowest risk. The Opportunity Consultant must have leadership skills and

demonstrate a strong understanding of On Demands Configurable Service offerings, outsourcing services, third party products and

services, contract structures, various costing/pricing methods, and various approaches to terms and conditions to craft large, configurable

On Demand Solutions. The Opportunity Consultant shall take a leadership role in convincing the customer contacts of the creditability

of the solution and effectiveness of the solution in meeting the customer’s requirements. The Opportunity Consultant shall have

interaction with middle to senior customer contacts. (Example: CIO, CFO, Operations Managers, Legal, Outside Consultants and Legal

Firms).

Responsibilities

• During the sales cycle, engages with the OD Sales Rep and OD Opportunity Consultants to form the Deal Team leadership. The threeperson

team will held accountable for the success of the sale as well as validity or “health” of the overall solution. The Opportunity Consultant

will provide customer-facing activities such as presenting the value proposition to the Customer’s C-Level as well as the tactical level value

proposition to the Customers mid-level management team.

• Responsible for creating the operational solution that balances the needs of the solution, customer, and Oracle. The operational solution

will require proactive and strategic thought in the selection of various configurable services that need to be combined to form a complete and

viable operational/services strategy.

• Responsible for creating the costing and risk models that balance the needs of the solution, customer, and Oracle. The costing and services

solution will require strategic and tactical thought in the identification of cost drivers of the solution. Proactive and efficient communication is

needed in order to gather the costing data and customer requirements from both internal and external sources. Various costing models from

Oracle’s LOB need to be combined and rationalized in order form a complete and deliverable costing strategy.

• Support the Opportunity Consultant and Oracle legal in the contract negotiation of the solution with the Customer’s negotiation team. The

Engagement Executive must resolve key concerns regarding the SOW and must facilitate resolution due to the operational impacts caused by

T&C changes. Such T&C changes include but not limited to: Data Privacy, Security, IP Rights, Remedies, and SLA Credits. The

Customer’s negotiation team will include one or all of the following: CIO, Procurement, Legal Counsel, Outside Legal Counsel, and or

Outside Consulting firms such as TPI.

• Responsible for taking the complete operational solution through Oracle’s approval process which includes various Oracle’s LOB

approval streams as well as corporate approvals from such organizations as; Revenue Recognition, Risk Management, and

HQAPP. The Opportunity Consultant will have the lead of documenting and prioritizing the approval concerns related to the

operational issues and working with the Deal Team to resolve those concerns. This will include multiple cycles back through the approval

process. This must be accomplished with speed and effectiveness in order to keep pace with the Sales cycle timeline.

• Change Agent. Based on “real deal” experiences, creates recommendations for changes in Configurable Services, costing

methods, and terms & conditions.

• Provides education to OD Sales on how match Configurable Services with the Customer’s requirements.

• Provides OD Sales with insight to the value proposition of Oracle’s delivery of Services and how best to overcome common objections to

Oracle’s business practices.

Brief Description
Provides programs to improve operational efficiency, consistency, and compliance in support of the organization*s financial and tactical business objectives. Provides business practices and processes. Develops, communicates, and trains the organization on business practices and processes.
Detailed Description
Serve as a liaison with other divisions such as Finance, Contracts, HR, Legal, Shared Services, Accounts Receivable, Purchasing, and Risk Management in an effort to ensure accurate and timely transaction processing. Collect, input, verify, correct, and analyze data to measure key performance indicator actual versus business objectives. Provide updates to management regarding budget to actual, informing them of deviations and opportunities. Provide management with economic impact and compliance issues surrounding key business decisions and/or deals. Communicate Oracle Business Practices to the organization and monitor process and approvals for full compliance. Drive implementation of new processes and procedures.

Follow-through skills necessary to get information from internal and third parties and have data errors/omissions corrected. For this, relationship management skills strongly desired. Strong written and verbal communication skills . Ability to partner across functions. 10 plus years relevant work experience.
