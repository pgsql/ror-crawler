Department Description

Oracle Communications Information Development – Order Management team

 

Oracle Communications has an opening for a curriculum developer/trainer in Toronto, CA. The person in this role creates and revises curriculum and product documentation as well as builds training environments to be used to train Oracle employees, system integrators, and customers on the Oracle Communications Order and Service Management product and RODOD (Rapid Offer Design and Order Delivery) solution. The person in this role also  delivers training to internal Oracle audiences and system integrators. We are looking for people with extensive knowledge of and experience in the ordering sector of the telecommunications industry. In addition, it’s important that you have excellent information development, training, and research skills, and the flexibility to manage changing deadlines and priorities as well as the proven ability to meet deadlines, exercise good judgment to resolve problems, and create high-quality work.
Brief Description
Develops courseware to support new and existing products, often though not exclusively in conjunction with Product Division.
Detailed Description
Coordinate and evaluate development contributions from other courseware development team members. Develop certification questions for use in the Oracle Certified Professional (OCP) exams. Solicit & incorporate feedback from reviews and training events. Deliver training at train-the-trainer and pilot events.
Job Requirements
Acknowledged authority within the Corporation.  Acts as a leader of large-scale company initiatives.  Viewed by peers as a leader and top contributor and by line management as a key business partner.  Bachelor*s Degree in Computer Science or Instructional Design. Four years adult education experience (as teacher or curriculum developer). Ten years technical or professional experience using Oracle products or related software. Strong SQL and PL/SQL knowledge and experience. Knowledge of XML, Java, and J2EE technologies. Experience with Oracle9i Database administration. Knowledge of Oracle9i Application server.
Additional Details

As a member of the Information Development team, your role is to:

·         Deliver general telecommunications training to the Information Development team. This training will focus on the Order and Service Management and Design Studio products and their supporting software components (including Unix OS, Oracle Database, Oracle WebLogic Server), system installation and administration, use of all aspects of the products in a hands-on environment, creating cartridges to extend Order and Service Management, On an ongoing basis, mentor writers and curriculum developers on ordering, 3rd party software component, Order and Service Management specific topics, and general telecommunications topics, including eTOM components and flows.

·         Work with customers, systems integrators, and Oracle consultants, support engineers, and sales teams to identify product training requirements. 

·         Work with product leads, architects, designers, internal consultants and Information Development team members to understand new software features, the business scenarios and customer requirements which drive their development and be able to apply the information to materials you create, and to train internal employees on the details.

·         Design and develop curriculum to be used to train customers and systems integrators. This includes:

o    Creating new training courses, both online and instructor-led.

o    Developing a training environment and hands-on labs to support the material.

o    Delivering train-the-trainer sessions to instructors and assessing their ability to teach the materials in the future.

·         Coordinate and deliver order management training to systems integrators and internal Oracle teams such as Consulting, Customer Support, and Sales. This includes:

o    Delivering standup training using existing or newly created materials.

o    Coordinating training logistics with internal Oracle teams and Oracle system integrators (e.g., schedule, location, registration, etc.).

·         Review work with managers, editors, and reviewers and apply suggested changes accurately.

·         Understand the tools and guidelines used to create curriculum and the production processes used to create and publish courseware through Oracle University (the Oracle group responsible for delivering product training to our customers).

  • Complete work according to agreed schedules and organize own time efficiently.

·         Obtain feedback through course evaluations; change materials based on feedback trends and your observations of participants after the training.

  • Assess abilities of participants and recommend follow-on training where necessary.
