Job Title Technical Writer 4-ProdDev
Location STAMFORD
Organization Name EPM Information Development
Department Description

The EPM Documentation group is responsible for creating and maintaining client documentation for our EPM software.
Brief Description
Creates, develops, plans, writes and edits operational, instructional, maintenance, test or user manuals for paper, multimedia or web-based publications. Contributes to the timely design, production and delivery/completion of product documentation and document sets.
Detailed Description
As a member of the technical writing division, you will organize and prepare information for publication and develop publication concepts for the best communication of subject matter. Review technical information prepared by other staff members for clarity and content. Take an active role in the definition and evolution of standard practices and procedures.
Job Requirements
Work is non-routine and very complex, involving the application of advanced technical/business skills in area of specialization. Leading contributor individually and as a team member, providing direction and mentoring to others. BS or MS degree or equivalent experience relevant to functional area. 7 years of software engineering or related experience.
Additional Details

Principal Technical Writers are responsible for all aspects of a documentation project—from analyzing user needs to preparing and obtaining approval of documentation plans through final production. They work independently and often provide assistance to more junior Technical Writers. Principal Technical Writers have excellent application knowledge and are aware of business issues and how they impact overall project plans. Principal Technical Writers have superior documentation project management skills and are responsible for scheduling documentation, communicating risks and dependencies, and providing project status to management and other members of the Development teams as appropriate.

In addition to developing extremely high-quality documentation independently from start to finish, Principal Technical Writers play a key role in setting strategies and goals for the EPM Doc group and in implementing and tracking processes to meet these goals. Principal Technical Writers are involved in the Documentation industry as a whole and communicate upcoming trends and technologies to Documentation Managers and other Technical Writers as appropriate. On an ongoing basis, Principal Technical Writers are responsible for identifying process issues within the documentation team and recommending appropriate improvements. Principal Technical Writers take an active role in raising the quality and consistency of documentation deliverables and exhibit leadership in department-wide initiatives.
